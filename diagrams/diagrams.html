On-Call Paved Road

Deployment-safety demo: a metric-gated canary that automatically rolls back a bad release. The failure under test is a v2 that queries an incidents.severity column the database has not migrated to yet.

1 · The system

Architecture

Everything runs in one local kind cluster. A load generator keeps steady traffic flowing so a bad release surfaces in metrics within seconds. The amber safety loop is the mechanism: Prometheus feeds the AnalysisTemplate, which can abort the Rollout on its own, before any human is involved.

2 · The incident

Bad deploy then automatic rollback

The money shot

The one to watch. A v2 that needs a database column the schema does not have yet is rolled out to 40% of traffic. Its /incidents requests error out, pushing the overall error rate to ~24% against a 5% SLO, and Argo Rollouts aborts and restores v1 in roughly 30 seconds, with nobody paged. The red band is the failure; the green band is the recovery.

40% canary slice ~24% error rate 5% SLO budget ~30s to rollback 0 paged

The arc, step by step
  1. Deploy v2; Argo routes 40% of traffic to it as a canary.
  2. Every /incidents request touches the missing severity column and returns a 500.
  3. The canary's /incidents calls error out, pushing the overall rate to ~24% (not all canary traffic is /incidents), well above the 5% SLO budget.
  4. The AnalysisRun reads that from Prometheus and marks the canary Failed.
  5. Argo aborts and shifts 100% of traffic back to the healthy v1, in about 30 seconds. No page.

3 · The production path

Same manifests, on Azure

The same Rollout and AnalysisTemplate run unchanged in production. Only the platform underneath swaps out: kind becomes AKS, the Postgres pod becomes Azure Database for PostgreSQL, and Prometheus becomes Azure Monitor managed Prometheus.

CI/CD pipeline — commit to production

How a change reaches production safely. A push is built, tested, image-scanned, and migration-linted (the expand/contract check that prevents the exact failure above), pushed to the registry, and synced by Argo CD. The canary then either promotes to 100% or auto-rolls-back on an SLO breach.
